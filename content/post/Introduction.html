Welcome! I am a recent PhD graduate and aspiring Data Scientist, based in Cambridge, UK.

A bit about me - I completed my PhD in Statistics and Operational Research at the STOR-i Centre for Doctoral Training, looking at a variety of areas in time-series forecasting, including linking forecasts to inventory management, hierarchical forecasting, and modelling multiple seasonalities and calendar effects (like Christmas/Bank Holidays) in a retail setting. You can find my thesis here.

Most recently, I’ve been working as a Postdoctoral Research Assistant on the LHOFT project, a collaboration between a number of Universities and industrial partners to predict/optimise freight routes between the UK and destinations abroad, with a particular focus on the north of England. I really enjoyed this role, particularly as it involved drawing multiple datasets from different sources together and allowed to apply a range of knowledge from Stats and OR, for example combining current trends in forecasting with good old linear programming.

When I’m not working, I like to mess around on the piano (mainly jazz) and most sports. I also enjoy playing chess, where I am currently graded 2208 by the ECF.
